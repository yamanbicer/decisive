## Market Sizing Rubric

A credible market case is **bottom-up**, not "1% of a huge number."

1. **Bottom-up TAM** — (# reachable customers) × (annual contract value). Show the
   unit. Reject top-down "$900B grocery market" framings that hide the real wedge.
2. **SAM** — the slice you can actually sell to in 3 years given GTM and product.
3. **SOM** — a believable 12–24 month capture, tied to the GTM motion's throughput.
4. **Growth & timing** — Is the market growing, and why is NOW the moment (a tech,
   regulatory, or behavior shift)? A great market at the wrong time still fails.
5. **Willingness to pay** — Evidence of budget and a buyer, not just interest.

## Evidence to fetch
Use `market_research` / `fetch_url` to pull: comparable companies' funding and
valuation, public revenue multiples, and category growth rates. Cite the source.

## Red flags
- TAM cited with no unit economics underneath.
- "If we just get 1% of X" reasoning.
- No identified budget-holding buyer.

## How to apply
Rebuild the number bottom-up in one line, compare to the founder's claim, and move
your score toward whichever survives scrutiny.
