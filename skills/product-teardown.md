## Product Teardown Rubric

Score the product on five axes (0–10 each); a strong product clears 7+ on at least three.

1. **Job-to-be-done** — Is there a sharp, frequent, painful job? Vitamin vs painkiller.
   Strong: a job the user already pays/works to do today. Weak: a "nice to have."
2. **Wedge** — The narrow first use case that wins a beachhead. Strong: one workflow
   owned end-to-end. Weak: a broad platform with no entry point.
3. **Moat / defensibility** — What stops a well-funded competitor (or one good model)
   from cloning it in a weekend? Data network effects, integrations, switching costs,
   regulatory moat. Weak: a thin UI over a public API/model.
4. **Adoption loop** — How does usage compound? Retention, virality, expansion.
   Look for week-4 retention, WAU/MAU, and whether the loop is intrinsic.
5. **Time-to-value** — How fast does a new user reach the "aha"? Minutes beats weeks.

## Red flags
- Conflates three products into one pitch (no focus).
- "TAM = the whole market" top-down sizing with no bottom-up wedge.
- Retention below category bar dressed up as "early."

## How to apply
Pick the ONE axis that most determines this product's fate, ground it in evidence
(use a tool to check competitors/traction), and let it move your score decisively.
